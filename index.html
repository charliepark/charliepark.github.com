Hey there!

I’m a frontend engineer in Oakland, California. I’m currently at LaunchDarkly. Previously, I was an engineer at Slack and IFTTT. I also founded and ran a personal finance startup for several years.

I love a lot about LaunchDarkly, but have been here for a few years and am open to new opportunities.

Here are some of the things I’m thinking about when considering where I want to work.

People

  • Are the people kind? Humble? Friendly?
  • Is the team diverse? Are the founders and execs women and/or people of color? (This one is really important to me.)
  • Do people from under-represented groups feel like their voices are heard? Are their voices sought out / amplified?
  • Do people lift one another up, and do they help everyone improve? Are they quick to share what they know? Can people communicate well?

Process

  • Is the company’s culture healthy and do the values mean something to them? Will they commit to their values even if it means they might lose business?
  • Are there healthy manager / IC relationships?
  • Does the product development team actively seek input from the support team, to better understand their customers’ needs?

Product

  • Does the product make the world a better place?
  • Is the UX delightful?
  • Does the team prioritize accessibility, so everyone can use their product?
  • (Not a hard requirement, but do they have a Content Designer / UX Writer on the team? At the very least, do they understand that words are a critical part of good design?)

I’d love to chat if you think we might be a match. You can reach out on LinkedIn. (Please mention something from the above list so I know you’re not just reaching out cold.) Have a great day!
