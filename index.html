About

I’m an indie web developer, with about 20 years in the web world. Most of that time’s been spent running my own bootstrapped webapp, or working as a front-end engineer at various startups; most recently, Slack.

I live in San Francisco, CA, with my wife, our three badass daughters, and our mini dachshund, Fern.

Writing

I’m hoping to get my blog back up, but you know how that goes. In the meantime, here are a few things I’ve written.

Slopegraphs: My most-read piece, about an overlooked style of data visualization created by Edward Tufte. After I published it, slopegraph use exploded.
Edward Tufte himself subsequently said that I was “slim and elegant”, but I like to think I just have a high data-to-ink ratio. Ugh, I’m sorry. Could be worse, though. The only thing worse than having to hear a big dumb dataviz joke is having to hear small multiples.

Santa is a Game People Play: How we did Christmas with our kids. It worked well. tl;dr: treat your kids like friends who you want to get in on the joke.

Unity: A recent one! A “preimagining” I wrote before the March 15th Democratic Debate. It didn’t exactly play out like this, but I’m still clinging to hope that Elizabeth Warren will save us all.

Colophon

This design was inspired by a 1988 brochure from the EPA’s Solid Waste and Emergency Department. It’s a reminder of a kinder, gentler, nobler time, when the US government planned ahead for crises and didn’t kill its citizens through its own gross incompetence.
